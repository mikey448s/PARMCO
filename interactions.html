> Let’s get this PARMCO prototype wired up. When building a mixed-voltage system (12V, 5V, 3.3V) for an ECSE 4235 project, breadboard organization is everything. One misplaced 12V jumper will instantly fry your Raspberry Pi 4. Here is a step-by-step, logical breakdown to map your netlist to a physical breadboard. 1. Establish the Power Rails (Crucial Step) You have three different voltages. Use the long rails on the sides of your breadboard carefully. Bottom Red Rail: Designate this as +12V (Connect Barrel Jack V+ here). Top Red Rail: Designate this as +5V (Connect RP4 Pin 2 here). Any unused short section: Designate as +3.3V (Connect RP4 Pin 1 here). All Blue/Black Rails: Connect them ALL together to form your COMMON_GND. Connect the Barrel Jack GND and the RP4 Pin 6 here. Add the Bulk Capacitor: Plug your 470uF capacitor (C1) directly into the +12V rail (long leg) and COMMON_GND rail (short leg/striped side). 2. Place the Main Components L293D: Push this into the middle of the breadboard so it straddles the center trench. 4N25: Push this across the center trench as well. IRFZ34N: Place this flat-side facing you in a blank section of the board. The pins from left to right are Gate (1), Drain (2), Source (3). 3. Wire the Power Enable Switch (Optocoupler & MOSFET) This isolates the Pi from the 12V side and acts as your hardware safety shutoff. 4N25 Input (Pi Side): Connect a 100Ω resistor (R_OC_LED) from RP4 Pin 12 to 4N25 Pin 1. Connect 4N25 Pin 2 directly to COMMON_GND. 4N25 Output (12V Side): Connect 4N25 Pin 5 directly to the +12V rail. Connect 4N25 Pin 4 to the IRFZ34N Gate (Pin 1). MOSFET Connections: Connect a 1KΩ resistor (R_GATE_PD) from the IRFZ34N Gate (Pin 1) to COMMON_GND. Connect the IRFZ34N Source (Pin 3) to COMMON_GND. Leave the IRFZ34N Drain (Pin 2) alone for a moment—this is your SWITCHED_GND. 4. Wire the Motor Driver (L293D) Logic Power: Connect L293D Pin 16 (VCC1) to the +5V rail. Motor Power: Connect L293D Pin 8 (VCC2) to the +12V rail. The Switched Grounds: Connect L293D Pins 4, 5, 12, and 13 together. Also connect L293D Pin 9 (3,4EN) to this group. Run a jumper from this entire group to the IRFZ34N Drain (Pin 2). (Do not connect these to the common ground rail). RP4 Control Pins: Connect L293D Pin 1 (1,2EN) to RP4 Pin 22. Connect L293D Pin 2 (1A) to RP4 Pin 16. Connect L293D Pin 7 (2A) to RP4 Pin 18. 5. Wire the Motor & Filters Motor Terminals: Connect L293D Pin 3 (1Y) to one side of the DC motor. Connect L293D Pin 6 (2Y) to the other side of the DC motor. Brush Noise Filters: Plug one 100nF capacitor (C_NODE_A) from L293D Pin 3 to COMMON_GND. Plug the other 100nF capacitor (C_NODE_B) from L293D Pin 6 to COMMON_GND. 6. Wire the IR RPM Sensors Sensor 1 (Primary): VCC to the +3.3V rail. (Do NOT use 5V or you will damage the Pi's input pins). GND to COMMON_GND. DO (Data Out) to RP4 Pin 32. Sensor 2 (Match Mode): VCC to the +3.3V rail. GND to COMMON_GND. DO to RP4 Pin 36. Final check: Before you plug in the Pi or the 12V supply, take a multimeter and check for continuity between your +12V rail, +5V rail, +3.3V rail, and COMMON_GND. None of them should beep!
