> I know exactly why it stopped reading, and it is a fascinating hardware logic trap! The culprit is the difference between how Python's bouncetime=2 works and how C's gpioGlitchFilter(PIN_IR, 1000) works. Python's Bouncetime: Registers the sensor the instant the fan blade hits it, and then ignores any static noise for 2 milliseconds. C's Glitch Filter: Waits to see if the signal lasts for at least 1 millisecond. If the signal is shorter than 1ms, it pretends nothing happened. As your motor speeds up to 2000+ RPM, the fan blades are spinning so incredibly fast that they block the IR sensor for less than 1 millisecond. The C program's glitch filter thinks the fast-moving blade is just "electronic noise" and deletes the reading! That is why the RPM suddenly drops to 0 at higher speeds. To fix this, I have removed the gpioGlitchFilter and replaced it with a manual software debounce inside the ir_sensor_callback. It now behaves exactly like Python's bouncetime.
